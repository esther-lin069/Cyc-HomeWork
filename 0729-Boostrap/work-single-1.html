Cockatoo

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Cockatoos have a large bill, which is kept sharp by rasping the two mandibles together when resting.
The bill is complemented by a large muscular tongue which helps manipulate seeds inside the bill so that they can be de-husked before eating.
During the de-husking, the lower mandible applies the pressure, the tongue holds the seed in place and the upper mandible acts as an anvil.
The eye region of the skull is reinforced to support muscles which move the mandibles sideways.
The bills of male cockatoos are generally slightly larger than those of their female counterparts, but this size difference is quite marked in the palm cockatoo.
